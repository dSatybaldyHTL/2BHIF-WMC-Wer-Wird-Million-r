    Eckhard Freise

    The first winner of the million in the German version of "Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?" was Eckhard Freise, a historian from Paderborn. He won on December 2, 2000, by correctly answering the question: "With whom did Edmund Hillary stand on the summit of Mount Everest in 1953?" with the answer "Tenzing Norgay".

    Marlene Grabherr

    Marlene Grabherr, a housewife from Allgäu, was the second millionaire in the show. She won on May 20, 2001, by correctly answering the question: "Which two Gibb brothers of the pop band The Bee Gees are twins?" with the answer "Maurice and Robin".

    Gerhard Krammer

    The third winner was Gerhard Krammer, a philosophy student and musician from Bavaria. He won on October 18, 2002, by correctly answering the question: "Which famous writer, as a trained architect, built a swimming pool in Zurich?" with the answer "Max Frisch".

    Maria Wienströer

    Dr. Maria Wienströer, a doctor from Münster, became the fourth millionaire in the show. She won on March 29, 2004, by correctly answering the question: "Who received both the Chemistry and the Nobel Peace Prize in 1954 and 1962?" with the answer "Linus Pauling".

    Stefan Lang

    Stefan Lang, an elevator installer from Nuremberg, became the fifth millionaire. He won on October 9, 2006, by correctly answering the question: "Which chemical element makes up more than half of the mass of the human body?" with the answer "Oxygen".

    Timur Hahn

    Timur Hahn, a media studies student, won the million on January 8, 2007. His correct answer to the question: "Which sea is named after a mythical king who is said to have thrown himself into it?" was "Aegean Sea".

    Oliver Pocher

    Comedian Oliver Pocher won the million on May 30, 2008, in the celebrity special. The million-dollar question was: "The Nail-Schreckenberg model provides an explanation for the occurrence of...?" and he answered correctly with "Traffic Jams".

    Thomas Gottschalk

    TV host Thomas Gottschalk became the eighth winner on November 20, 2008. His million-dollar question was: "Who is said to have invented the electric light bulb?" and he answered correctly with "Thomas Edison".

    Ralf Schnoor

    Café owner Ralf Schnoor won the million on November 26, 2010. The million-dollar question was: "Which famous historical figure built the first German parliament?" and he correctly answered "Otto von Bismarck".

    Barbara Schöneberger

    Barbara Schöneberger won the million on May 30, 2011. She correctly answered the million-dollar question: "What is the name of the closest star to the Earth?" with "The Sun".

    Sebastian Langrock

    Poker player Sebastian Langrock won the million on March 11, 2013, after correctly answering the million-dollar question: "Which city is known for its large-scale, famous carnival?" with "Rio de Janeiro".

    Thorsten Fischer

    Thorsten Fischer, a forest house operator from North Rhine-Westphalia, won the million on October 17, 2014, by answering: "What is the name of the most famous German opera house?" with the correct answer "Berlin State Opera".

    Nadja Sidikjar

    Dr. Nadja Sidikjar, a pediatrician from Berlin, became a millionaire on November 13, 2015, by correctly answering the million-dollar question: "What is the common name for the fifth element in the periodic table?" with "Boron".

    Leon Windscheid

    Psychologist Leon Windscheid won the million on December 7, 2015. The question was: "What color does a frog turn when exposed to stress?" and the correct answer was "Green".

    Jan Stroh

    Insurance salesman Jan Stroh won the million on September 2, 2019. His million-dollar question was: "What word describes a clock that measures time precisely?" and his correct answer was "Atomic Clock".

    Ronald Tenholte

    Ronald Tenholte, a juice and smoothie bar owner, won the million on March 24, 2020, by answering: "Which artist painted the "Mona Lisa?" with the answer "Leonardo da Vinci".
