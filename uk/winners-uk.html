    Judith Keppel

    The first million-pound winner in the UK version of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? was Judith Keppel, a garden designer from London. She won the million on November 20, 2000, by correctly answering the question: "Which king was married to Eleanor of Aquitaine?" with "Henry II".

    David Edwards

    The second winner of the UK version was David Edwards, a physics teacher from Staffordshire. He won the million on April 21, 2001, by correctly answering the question: "If you planted the seeds of Quercus robur, what would grow?" with "Trees" (Oak trees).

    Robert Brydges

    The third winner was Robert Brydges, a former investment banker. He won the million on September 29, 2001, by correctly answering the question: "Which scientific unit is named after an Italian nobleman?" with "Volt".

    Pat Gibson

    The fourth winner was Pat Gibson, an Irish software developer and quiz player. He won the million on April 24, 2004, by correctly answering the question: "Which of these is not one of the American Triple Crown horse races?" with "Arlington Million".

    Ingram Wilcox

    The fifth winner was Ingram Wilcox, a retired civil servant. He won the million on September 23, 2006, by correctly answering the question: "Which scientific word means 'snail-like' in Greek?" with "Cochlea".

    Donald Fear

    The sixth winner was Donald Fear, a history teacher from Shropshire. He won the million on September 11, 2020, by correctly answering the question: "In 1718, which pirate died in battle off the coast of what is now North Carolina?" with "Blackbeard".
